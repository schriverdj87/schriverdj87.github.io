Tools

Deck Simulator

Create and simulate a deck of cards.

A time-saver for those looking to develop card games (or money-saver for those who don't want to buy a physical game). Click the cheeseburger and enter your deck as plain text (within the app, instructions on how to do this will be in the console output, just press F12 and open the console tab). Here's an example of a simple memory game: Memory|Apple:2|Banana:2|Grapes:2|Pear:2|Stinkbug:2. Then click “Layout as Deck” or “Layout as Grid” (latter for the example). Click a card once to flip it, click and drag to move it. Be advised that this does not work on mobile devices.

Try it out!

Flex Sampler

Interactively tinker with and see flexbox css rules in action.

This is an essential tool for anyone working with flexbox in front-end web design that I have used myself in various projects. Everything in green text is a CSS attribute that works with flexbox. Just play around with the drop down menus and number fields until you get what you're looking for then use that to write your CSS.

Try it out!

Interval Timer

Continuously counts down and plays a sound at regular intervals.

Intended as an aid for those with attention problems, but is also useful as a reminder when something needs to be done at regular intervals. Just pick your settings including interval, sound played, and background, and click start. The application will display a timer against a scrolling background and will play a sound and start over when it reaches 0. Click anywhere to return to the menu. Be advised that it is ideal that this remains on screen as the browser may pause the program if it thinks it isn't in use.

Try it out!

Template Sandwich

Generate text with repeating elements.

Examples of where this would be useful would include making repetitive SQL queries separated by “union” or hard-coded repeating HTML elements. On the top bun write the template with [REP1] for the element that changes each time. On the bottom bun write the part that changes each time separated by commas. The meat and cheese shows the output.

Try it out!

Unit of Measure App

Dynamically convert one unit of measure into multiple.

More dynamic (and entertaining) than converting prompts available in search engines. Just pick your starting unit of measure, type in the number you want to convert and click the white background to see its equals in other units of measure.

Try it out!

Games

Alternate Alphabet Teacher

Learn standard galactic alphabet, alienese, braille, and more!

A great way to learn to read and write in various codes. Just pick the language you want start the game and type in the right letters as long as you like. “Speed Up” mode offers a typing challenge for those who have learned the basic alphabet. Does not work on mobile.

Try it out!

Memory Training

Memorize the current animal while inputting a previous one!

Inspired by exercises from “Brain Age Concentration Training.” Once you hit “Start” you will be shown a sequence of animals. Use the animal pictures to punch in that sequence WHILE remembering the one currently being displayed. You can use the options and presets to customize your game to an acceptable challenge. Might be useful for clearing your head.

Try it out!

Space Treasure

Try to find all 4 treasures in a grid full of enemies and hazards!

Once you get the hang of it, it's good for a quick break. Pay close attention to the text output at the top. Click on accessable tiles to guide the green sheepie around the grid. Use various weapons to fight enemies you encounter (hint: each weapon corresponds with an enemy and will take out that enemy in one hit.). Find all 4 treasures to win the game. The game changes for every weekday and you can even make your own custom games!

Try it out!
